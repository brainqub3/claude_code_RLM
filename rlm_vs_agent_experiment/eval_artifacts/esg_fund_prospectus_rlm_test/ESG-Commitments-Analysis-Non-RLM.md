# ESG Commitments Across BlackRock Investment Funds

**Source:** *BlackRock Investment Funds — Prospectus, 15 June 2026* (FCA PRN 664232)
**Analysis date:** 19 June 2026
**Scope:** All 27 sub-funds described in Appendix 1 of the prospectus.

---

## 1. Executive summary

Of the 27 sub-funds in the Trust, **14 make a specific, disclosed ESG commitment** in their investment objective/policy. The remaining funds rely only on BlackRock's firm-wide *ESG integration* (a research input, **not** a binding commitment) and are therefore excluded from the ESG group below.

The 14 ESG funds fall into three structural families, and they differ sharply on the **three dimensions asked about**:

| Dimension | What differs |
|---|---|
| **Sustainability label (UK SDR)** | Only **one** fund carries an SDR label (Brown to Green Materials = *Sustainability Improvers*). All other 13 explicitly state they have **no** UK sustainable investment label, despite applying ESG commitments. |
| **Benchmarks** | Range from **cash + alpha target benchmarks** (the absolute-return bond/credit funds), to **equity-index comparators** (Brown to Green), to **carbon-only "Reference Comparators"** (MyMap/LifePath fund-of-funds), to **no benchmark at all** (MyMap Select ESG / LifePath, which use a Morningstar peer category instead). |
| **Risk factors** | All share a common "Risks relating to the application of ESG criteria" block (§24(c)(viii)). Carbon-targeting funds add a **GHG/Scope-3 data risk** factor; the labelled fund (Brown to Green) adds **transition/"improver"-specific risks**; fund-of-funds add **underlying-fund ESG-compatibility risk**. |

## 4. Dimension-by-dimension analysis

### 4.1 Sustainability labels

- **Only Brown to Green Materials Fund uses a UK SDR label** — the **Sustainability Improvers** label. It qualifies because it has an explicit *sustainability objective*: investing in assets "that may not be sustainable now, with an aim to improve their sustainability… over time," determined against a "robust, evidence-based standard" (BlackRock's proprietary **SDR Improver Assessment**, requiring ≥70% of assets to be "Transition Improvers").

- **All 13 other ESG funds explicitly disclaim a label**, with two distinct rationales:
  - **Single-strategy funds** (Sterling Strategic Bond, ESG Screened & Selected, both Credit funds): *"the investment strategy of the Fund means it is not able to meet the criteria of any sustainability label."* — i.e. they apply ESG commitments but have no specific sustainability *goal*.
  - **Fund-of-funds** (MyMap Select ESG, LifePath): *"the underlying funds in which the Fund invests may not meet the criteria of any sustainability label."* — i.e. the fund-of-funds structure itself blocks labelling.

> **Takeaway:** Applying ESG criteria ≠ qualifying for an SDR label. 13 of 14 ESG funds apply real commitments yet remain unlabelled.

### 4.2 Benchmarks

The ESG funds use benchmarks for **three different purposes**, and many distinguish a *performance* benchmark from a separate *ESG/carbon* benchmark:

1. **Absolute-return / "cash + spread" target benchmarks** — the income-oriented funds set a return target *over* a cash rate:
   - Sterling Strategic Bond: **3-mo SONIA + 4.5%**
   - Sterling Short Duration Credit: **3-mo SONIA + 1.5%** (+ iBoxx GBP Corporates 0-5 comparator)
   - ESG Screened & Selected: **BoE Base Rate + 4.5%**
   - These funds *also* name a **separate, non-ESG index** purely to measure the carbon-intensity reduction (e.g. Bloomberg Global Aggregate Corporate; ICE BAML 1-5 Yr Global Corporate) — chosen because it represents the universe "without the application of any ESG criteria."

2. **ESG-tilted index inside the target** — Systematic Multi Allocation Credit's target composite is one-third **J.P. Morgan EMBI ESG Global Diversified**, so its ESG view is embedded in the performance benchmark itself.

3. **Equity-index comparators** — Brown to Green Materials is actively managed and *unconstrained* but offers **MSCI ACWI** (3–5 yr) and **MSCI ACWI Materials** (1–2 yr, also used for active-risk management).

4. **No benchmark / peer-category comparison** — the multi-asset fund-of-funds:
   - **MyMap Select ESG (3/5/8):** *no* target, constraining or comparator benchmark; performance judged against staying inside a **volatility risk profile** (3–6% / 8–11% / 12%+). A **"Reference Comparator"** (a non-ESG market-cap index blend matching the fund's asset mix) is used **only** to measure carbon intensity.
   - **LifePath:** "not managed to a benchmark"; performance compared to a **Morningstar EAA Fund Target Date** peer category that steps up by vintage:

     | Fund | Morningstar EAA Fund Target Date category |
     |---|---|
     | LifePath Retirement | 2021–2025 |
     | LifePath 2030 | 2026–2030 |
     | LifePath 2035 | 2031–2035 |
     | LifePath 2040 | 2036–2040 |
     | LifePath 2045 | 2041–2045 |
     | LifePath 2050 / 2055 / 2060 / 2065 | 2046+ |

### 4.3 Risk factors

**Common to every ESG fund** — §24(c)(viii) *"Risks relating to the application of ESG criteria"*:
- **ESG criteria vs performance** — may forgo otherwise-attractive opportunities or force divestment of holdings whose ESG characteristics change, even when financially disadvantageous.
- **Third-party data risk** — dependence on ESG data that may be incomplete, inaccurate, estimated, lagged (annual corporate reporting), inconsistent between providers, or low-coverage; data providers give no warranty; data errors can cause gains or losses borne by unitholders.
- **Changing ESG regulation & market practice** — sustainable investing is "an evolving space"; BlackRock may update ESG criteria/data sources as UK regulation evolves; demand for ESG products may shift.
- **Securities-lending collateral** may not comply with the fund's ESG criteria.

**Additional, fund-type-specific risks:**

- **Carbon-targeting funds** (Sterling Strategic Bond, Sterling Short Duration Credit, the MyMap Select ESG carbon commitment) reference a dedicated **GHG / carbon-emission-intensity risk factor** (§24(a)(xxii)/(xxiii)): carbon-intensity scores cover **Scope 1 & 2 but exclude Scope 3** emissions, so the score "may not be fully representative" of the issuers' true GHG footprint.

- **The labelled fund (Brown to Green)** carries the most distinct, transition-specific risk profile: it holds companies "that may not be sustainable now," depends on **forward-looking metrics** (SBTi targets, MSCI Implied Temperature Rise — based on projected/estimated data), on KPI "on-track" assessments, and on a discretionary **sustainable-investment sub-committee**. It may also retain companies that fall off track (moved to a *Climate Watchlist*; only declassified after **3 consecutive years** of no progress), and is **not prohibited from holding fossil-fuel-value-chain companies** where they fit the transitioning-materials theme.

- **Fund-of-funds (MyMap Select ESG, LifePath)** add **"Application of ESG criteria by underlying funds"** risk: BlackRock cannot guarantee the compatibility of an underlying scheme's holdings with the stated ESG criteria, and may continue to hold a CIS that has ceased to be an appropriate ESG investment.

## 5. Key nuance — "binding commitment" vs "monitoring"

The single most important distinction *within* the multi-asset ESG funds:

- **MyMap 3/5/8 Select ESG** make a **binding ESG commitment** on carbon: a portfolio carbon-emission-intensity score **30% below** the Reference Comparator, **plus an absolute reduction** versus the level five years prior (first measured 31 Mar 2028 vs 31 Mar 2023). The prospectus stresses that despite the binding commitment, "there can be no guarantee that these outcomes will be achieved."

- **LifePath funds** apply ESG commitments to fund *selection* (the BB+ government-rating and ESG-criteria thresholds) but only **monitor** the portfolio's ESG score and carbon intensity against the Reference Comparator — *"the Manager does not commit to improving the 'ESG score' or 'carbon emission intensity score' of the Fund's portfolio over time."*

This is the difference between an *outcome* pledge (MyMap Select ESG) and a *process + transparency* pledge (LifePath), even though neither carries an SDR label.
